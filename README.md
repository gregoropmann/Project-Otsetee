# Projekt Otsetee: Nutikas ja ohutu tee värske toiduni

Igal suvel täituvad Eesti teeperved isetehtud siltidega, mis kutsuvad möödasõitjaid maasikaid ostma, talupoodi sisse põikama või värsket suitsukala proovima. Kohaliku toidu väärtustamine on imeline, kuid füüsiliste siltide püstitamine riigiteede äärde peidab endas tõsiseid väljakutseid.

## Probleem: Füüsiliste siltide oht ja bürokraatia
Transpordiameti andmetel seab loata ja valesti paigaldatud teeperve reklaam ohtu liiklusohutuse.

"Probleem tekib olukorras, kus silt on paigaldatud kohta, kus sõidukijuht märkab seda liiga hilja. See võib kaasa tuua ootamatu pidurdamise või ohtliku manöövri teel," selgitab Transpordiameti järelevalve osakonna juhataja Siim Jaksi.

### Seadus nõuab teekaitsevööndisse (kuni 50 meetrit maanteest) siltide paigaldamiseks ametlikku kooskõlastust. Eestimaa Talupidajate Keskliidu juhatuse liikme Timo Varblase sõnul on see aga väiketootjatele liigne bürokraatia:

### "Loa taotlemise protsessi ja ooteaja peale võib lühike maasikahooaeg juba läbi saada, kusjuures maasikad tuleb niikuinii kiiresti maha müüa."

## Lahendus: Otsetee viib sildid teepervelt nutitelefoni
Projekt Otsetee sündis täpselt selle konflikti lahendamiseks. Meie eesmärk on asendada ohtlikud, illegaalsed ja segadust tekitavad puidust teeperve sildid ühe kaasaegse, reaalajas toimiva digitaalse kaardiga.

### 100% Ohutu liiklejatele:
Autojuhid ja kaasreisijad näevad teeäärseid müügipunkte otse rakendusest enne reaalset teeotsa. Ei mingeid ootamatuid pidurdamisi ega tähelepanu hajumist – vaid sujuv ja planeeritud peatuse tegemine.

### 0% Bürokraatiat talunikele:
Müüjad ei pea taotlema lube ega ootama nädalaid. Nad saavad oma müügipunkti ja tooted (maasikad karbis, mesi purgis, kartul kiloga) märkida kaardile sekunditega – täpselt siis, kui kaup on valmis ja hooajaline müük algab.

#### Kaitseme kõige nõrgemaid:
Teeperved peavad jääma vabaks ja ohutuks. Siltide asemel saavad autojuhid keskenduda sellele, mis on teel tõeliselt tähtis – jalakäijatele, lastele ja jalgratturitele.

## Otsetee missioon
Me usume, et teeäärne kaubandus on Eesti maaelu ja suve pärl. Otsetee annab kohalikele tootjatele digitaalse hääle ja ostjatele mugava teejuhi, muutes samal ajal meie maanteed kõigi jaoks turvalisemaks. Me ühendame kohaliku toidu värskuse ja liikluse turvalisuse, luues nutika otsetee tootjalt tarbijani.

### 1. Core Concept &amp; Vision
The Otsetee (or &quot;Osta-tee&quot;) application is designed to solve a critical, real-world conflict
between local agricultural commerce and transit highway regulations. In countries like Estonia,
the Transport Administration (Transpordiamet) mandates complex and time-consuming permit
processes for physical roadside signs placed near state highways. These restrictions exist to
ensure drivers are not distracted and sudden braking risks are prevented. However, for micro-
merchants operating on brief seasonal lifespans (e.g., wild berry picking, mushroom foraging, or
honey production), the bureaucratic delay renders traditional signage impossible.
The application serves as a dedicated digital alternative, shifting physical roadside promotion
into an active, real-time map network that eliminates the requirement for hardware permits while
dramatically improving public traffic safety.
### Brand Strategy &amp; Meaning
The app leverages a strategic linguistic double-meaning for the local Estonian market:
1. Otsetee: Represents a direct shortcut from the farm/forest to the commuter.
2. Osta-tee: Acts as an immediate behavioral trigger meaning &quot;buy and make on your route.&quot;

### 2. Operational Workflow
#### 2.1 The Merchant Interface (The &quot;Pop-Up&quot; Stall)
The system is engineered specifically for non-technical users, temporary seasonal merchants,
and rural wild-product harvesters. The merchant pipeline involves four rapid stages:
 Authentication: The seller securely logs into the application using single-click Google or
Apple identity profiles. No password creations or complex registration protocols are required.
 Initialization: Upon setting up a physical roadside site, the merchant launches the mobile
map screen and triggers a prominent single-action button labeled &quot;Start Selling&quot;.
 Telemetry Mapping: The application queries the mobile device&#39;s integrated GPS hardware,
pinning the merchant&#39;s precise location on the public directory map instantly.
 Inventory &amp; Pricing Selection: The merchant checks basic categorized toggles outlining
current inventory (e.g., Wild Chanterelles, Farm Strawberries) along with localized pricing
metrics, then updates their status to &quot;Live&quot;.
When inventory is sold out or the merchant finishes operation for the afternoon, they toggle the
session off, removing their active coordinates from the user database securely.

#### 2.2 The Consumer Interface (The Commuter/Driver)
Reading small cardboard or painted signs at highway speeds (90 km/h or higher) introduces
severe traffic risks and erratic braking behavior. The consumer client side addresses this
directly through pre-emptive route tracking:
 Pre-Trip Mapping: Before starting a transit route, consumers review an interactive live map
highlighting verified active merchant stalls currently deployed along their planned trajectory.
 Proximity Integration Alerts: Drivers or passengers can set up automated voice or push
notification parameters. As the transit vehicle approaches an active, verified stall location,
the device delivers a real-time speech warning (e.g., &quot;Local strawberry stall ahead in 1
kilometer on your right&quot;).
 Safe Pull-Over Margin: By receiving advanced digital notification, the vehicle operator
maintains a clear situational awareness buffer, allowing them to smoothly decrease velocity
and position the car off the main road lane safely.

### 3. Competitive Differentiation: Overcoming Social Media Limitations
Currently, many micro-merchants rely on localized community groups on platforms like
Facebook to advertise product batches. This approach contains terminal design failures:
 Information Decay: Static timeline updates degrade within minutes as newer feeds take
algorithmic precedence, making time-sensitive agricultural batches invisible.
 Context Blindness: Post updates lack dynamic geographic parameters. A driver cannot
cross-reference a plain-text status description with active route maps while safely
maintaining high-speed highway concentration.
 Data Cleanliness: Social media channels preserve outdated posts indefinitely, causing
drivers to navigate to long-abandoned stall sites.
By pairing clean data logic with real-time hardware GPS location updates, the Otsetee
ecosystem transforms unstructured roadside commerce into a synchronized, secure, and
entirely lawful regional trade network.
